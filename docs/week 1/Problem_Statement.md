# Week 1 — Problem Discovery  
## Problem Statement Document  

---

## 1. Problem Definition  
Adelaide Care Connect currently relies on manual processes such as paper-based forms, spreadsheets, and email communication to manage participants, staff scheduling, and service delivery.  

This leads to:
- Data inconsistency and duplication  
- Delays in service booking and communication  
- Lack of real-time visibility for staff and management  
- Poor user experience for participants  

---

## 2. Who Experiences the Problem?  

| User Group | Problem Faced |
|-----------|--------------|
| Participants (NDIS clients) | Cannot easily book or manage services online |
| Staff (Support Workers) | No central system to view or manage shifts |
| Administrators | Difficult to manage data, bookings, and staff efficiently |

---

## 3. Australian Market Context  

The system is designed within the **NDIS (National Disability Insurance Scheme)** environment in Australia.

Key considerations:
- NDIS providers must follow strict compliance and reporting standards  
- Increasing demand for digital solutions in disability services  
- Need for secure handling of sensitive participant data (Privacy Act 1988)  

---

## 4. Why is This Problem Important?  

- The NDIS sector supports thousands of Australians with disabilities  
- Inefficient systems can directly impact service quality and participant care  
- Digital transformation is essential for scalability and compliance  
- Improved systems can enhance both operational efficiency and user satisfaction  

---

## 5. Project Vision  

To develop a **secure, web-based platform** that:
- Centralises participant, staff, and service management  
- Enables online booking and scheduling  
- Improves communication between all stakeholders  
- Ensures compliance with Australian regulations  

---

## 6. Summary  

The project aims to solve inefficiencies in NDIS service management by introducing a centralized, secure, and user-friendly digital system tailored to the Australian healthcare support sector.
